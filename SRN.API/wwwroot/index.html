Strategic Research Nexus

An independent academic platform connecting Afghan researchers worldwide. We bridge academic divides to foster interdisciplinary collaboration and advance evidence-based research for Afghanistan's sustainable development.

Our Mission & Vision

To advance knowledge production and develop evidence-based research that empowers Afghan scholars. We envision a sustainable, inclusive, and flourishing Afghan society underpinned by equitable development, academic excellence, and technological innovation.

We are committed to the principles of equity, intellectual rigor, and ethical scholarship, guiding strategic interventions for academic institutions globally.

Research Focus

  • Digital Transformation & Technology: Enhancing digital service delivery, data integration, and promoting technological literacy in Afghanistan.
  • Culture & Literature: Preserving and analyzing Afghanistan’s rich cultural heritage and its role in shaping societal identity.
  • Sustainable Systems: Frameworks ensuring effective, inclusive, and resilient technological and infrastructure systems.
  • Environmental Sustainability: Supporting responsible use of natural resources, climate change mitigation, and ecological balance.

The SRN Team

A global network of dedicated academics and researchers.

Dr. Salim Saay

University of Limerick & Lero

Associate Professor and Researcher holding a Ph.D. in Software Engineering. Former faculty at Kabul University and IT leader in Afghan higher education. His research focuses on cross-border data integration, broker-based architectures, blockchain, and AI.

Dr. Abdul Aziz Mohibbi

Trinity College Dublin & Maynooth University

Agriculture & Environmental Engineer. Former Chancellor of Bamyan University and Deputy Dean at Kabul University, championing inclusive academic initiatives. His research focuses on environmental changes, co-design, and community-based systems innovation for regional sustainability.

Dr. Ahmad Farid Asadullahi

Haverford College

Assistant Professor holding a Ph.D. in Persian Literature. With extensive teaching experience at Kabul and Ghazni Universities, he actively supports Afghan community development and cultural preservation through various non-governmental organizations.
